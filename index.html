Marrakech

The Koutoubia Mosque is the largest religious building and the emblematic symbol of Marrakech. Erected in the 12th century, this marvel of Almohad architecture is distinguished by the sobriety and harmony of its style. Its imposing minaret, culminating at nearly 77 meters, serves as a visual landmark throughout the city.

Fez

Fez is the spiritual and cultural capital of Morocco. Located about 180 km east of Rabat, the city is famous for its historic medina, Fez el-Bali (classified by UNESCO), and for home to Al Quariawiyyin University, one of the oldest universities in the world still in operation.

Nador

Nador is a large coastal city in northeastern Morocco, ideally located in the Rif region on the Mediterranean Sea. It is located in the heart of the second largest lagoon in the Mediterranean, the Mar Chica. Cradle of Amazigh (Berber) culture, Nador has transformed into a dynamic economic center, driven by trade, port infrastructure and tourism development focused on ecology.
